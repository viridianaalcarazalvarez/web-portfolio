Artist Statement

My work begins with found materials, photographs, and small gestures of repetition. I build visual layers that shift between abstraction and fragments of landscape, inviting viewers to slow down and notice subtle traces of time.

Through paint, collage, and hand-altered surfaces, I treat the studio as a site of translation: memory becomes texture, and observation becomes form. I am interested in how places carry emotional residue and how images can hold both clarity and uncertainty at once.

Artist Statement

My work begins with memory—how it is held, altered, and re-encountered across physical and digital spaces. I’m drawn to the ways we store fragments of experience in objects, images, and systems that both preserve and distort what we remember.

Working across digital video, net art, digital painting, and traditional media, I move between the tactile and the digital. Hand-made marks are translated into digital environments where they can shift, fragment, and take on a kind of afterlife.

Drawing from personal experiences of childhood, loss, and emotion, I create work that functions as a personal archive of memories of the past.

Artworks

Painting

Push Towards the Sun, 2025

Acrylic on canvas

Push Towards the Sun is a painting that explores positivity, keeping a mindset of growth and hope. It is a reminder to keep on going, even when things feel dark.

Untitled (servilletas), 2025

Acrylic on canvas

This piece is based on a handmade crocheted servilleta my grandmother gave me before she passed. Even as dementia affected her memory, she continued crocheting—her hands moving with quiet persistence. It reflects on what remains when memory fades, and how making can carry a sense of presence, care, and spirit beyond words or recollection.

Recuerdame siempre, 2024

Acrylic on canvas

Recuerdame siempre depicts a portrait of Viridiana's mother layered with a drawing from her teenage years, marked with the words “Remember me forever”. The work reflects on memory and its fragility, and the fear of both forgetting and being forgotten.

Time Goes Fast, Love Always #1, 2024

Digital painting

This work is part of a project that culminated in the creation of a calendar, consisting of a series of digital paintings representing the seasons. The calendar is inspired by the long traditional Mexican calendars my family sends my mother each year from Mexico. It reflects on the joy and familiarity my mother feels receiving them, and how that shared gesture of connection resonates across immigrant families and beyond.

Video

lovely cloud, 2023

Digital video

Lovely Cloud is a stop-motion narrative made from digitized hand-drawn doodles. It gathers small personal moments into a short sequence and uses text from a roughly translated phrase found on an inexpensive phone case—imperfect language as part of the feeling.

Recuerdame, 2023

Digital video

Recuerdame, 2023 features hand-drawn text drawn from poems written by Viridiana's mother during her teenage years, alongside recognizable characters from Mexican popular culture, such as the Bimbo bread bear. While subtle in appearance, the work reflects deeply personal elements of her identity. It incorporates fragments of everyday life—references to family, cultural heritage, and memories she seeks to preserve.

Net Art

Blurred Echoes, 2025

Website

Blurred Echoes is an interactive website that presents my memories in an abstract, unreadable form. The work stems from my anxiety about losing memory and watching the experiences that shaped me fade into something unrecognizable. Through hazy images and glowing orbs, it explores the tension between holding on and letting go.

music-memories, 2025

Website

Music often reflects who a person is, carrying feelings, memories, and phases of life without needing explanation. My playlists, built over years on Apple Music, trace different periods of my life and are closely tied to memory and personal change. This webpage brings together my drawings and curated playlists as a way to share how music and visual work shape each other in my creative process.
